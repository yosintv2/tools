What is IPO (Initial Public Offering)?

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is when a private company offers its shares to the public for the first time and gets listed on the Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE). This allows ordinary investors like you to become part-owners of the company.

Types of Public Issues in Nepal

  • IPO – Initial Public Offering (first time)
  • FPO – Follow-on Public Offering
  • Right Share – Existing shareholders priority
  • Bonus Share – Free shares from profit

How to Apply for IPO in Nepal (2025 Guide)

  1. Open Demat Account
  2. Open ASBA-enabled bank account
  3. Get CRN Number
  4. Link CRN with Demat in MeroShare
  5. Apply via MeroShare.cdsc.com.np or bank app
Golden Tip: Apply on the first day within the first 2-3 hours for maximum allotment chance!

Why Invest in IPOs in Nepal?

  • Usually issued at ₨100 – huge listing gains
  • Hydropower & bank IPOs often give 100-500% return on day 1
  • 10% reserved for Nepalis abroad

Popular IPO Sectors in Nepal 2025

  • Hydropower (most IPOs)
  • Microfinance & Commercial Banks
  • Insurance Companies
  • Cement & Manufacturing
  • Hotels & Tourism
